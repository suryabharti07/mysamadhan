Empowering Women, Transforming Communities

Samadhan Kendra's Women Self Help Groups (WSHGs): A foundation for sustainable livelihoods.

Discover Our Impact

What Are Women Self Help Groups (WSHGs)?

A Women Self Help Group (WSHG) is a community-based group consisting of 10 or more women, typically from similar socio-economic backgrounds. These women come together with a shared goal: to save collectively, support each other financially, and build pathways to sustainable livelihoods.

The WSHG model not only provides financial support but also builds confidence, leadership skills, and solidarity among women who have historically been excluded from mainstream financial systems.

Core Focus: Financial Empowerment

Collective Savings

Women pool small savings on a regular basis to build a shared fund.

Internal Lending

The group provides low-interest loans to members for various needs and small businesses.

Bank Linkages

Groups are linked with banks for larger credit and financial literacy training.

This structure allows women to gain control over their finances, break free from dependency, and actively contribute to their families’ income and well-being.

Regular Support and Monitoring

Unlike informal groups, WSHGs associated with Samadhan Kendra benefit from consistent mentoring and guidance:

  • Monthly meetings are conducted by Samadhan Kendra staff, who visit each group to review records, offer financial guidance, and discuss community issues.
  • These sessions also provide training in areas like entrepreneurship, digital literacy, health awareness, and rights education.

Over time, these groups evolve into strong community institutions that can advocate for their own needs. This consistent engagement ensures that each group functions effectively and continues to grow in strength and purpose.

Real Impact: Changing Lives Across Communities

Over the years, WSHGs have had a life-changing impact on thousands of women and their families:

  • Women who once lacked confidence are now running small businesses, making household decisions, and sending their children to school.
  • They have become agents of change in their villages—addressing social issues, helping each other during crises, and leading community development activities.
  • Through access to credit and a strong support network, many women have broken the cycle of poverty and gained newfound dignity and independence.

These are not just financial groups—they are lifelines of transformation, creating ripple effects across entire communities.

WSHGs: A Foundation for Holistic Development

While the foundation of WSHGs lies in financial empowerment, their broader impact includes:

  • Social empowerment – giving women a voice in family and community matters.
  • Health and hygiene awareness – through group sessions and campaigns.
  • Skill development and entrepreneurship – enabling women to launch and sustain micro-enterprises.
  • Leadership development – with many women now participating in local governance and panchayati raj systems.

Conclusion

The Women Self Help Groups (WSHGs) of Samadhan Kendra are a powerful testament to what grassroots organization, consistent support, and community trust can achieve. Since 2004, these groups have been nurturing dreams, building leaders, and rewriting the narrative of rural women.

“When women rise, communities flourish.”

Samadhan Kendra’s WSHG initiative proves that empowering women is not only the right thing to do—it is the most impactful way to build a just, equitable, and prosperous society.
